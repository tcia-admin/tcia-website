Community of Practice

Join grassroot youth justice organizations to develop national youth digital justice policy agenda.

ABOUT

Develop critical digital literacy training for grassroots youth justice organizations to facilitate the development of a national youth digital justice policy agenda that safeguards students' fundamental rights and freedoms against digital pushout and youth surveillance. The national policy agenda will help recenter civil and human rights as the basis for AI governance and digital policy in public education and youth-serving systems at the local, state, and federal levels.

GOALS

Demystify the consequences of AI and data-driven technologies in the lives of marginalized youth

Develop a policy framework and legislative priorities that defend and expand rights and freedoms for our young people against AI and other data-driven technologies

Empower local, intergenerational advocacy for youth digital justice, especially through art, cultural, and narrative-based strategies

Grow and strengthen local infrastructures across the country to combat AI injustice and champion youth digital justice

Community Based Projects

  • Each organization will identify a way to relay their learning with their chosen community. Each org will receive a stipend for this project (not per person); a component of the stipend should be to activate an art/culture project, but it doesn’t need to be the whole stipend.
  • The hosts of the cohort have identified a queue of artists to support your efforts. Each org has access to this artist queue to select an “artist in residence” for their project. The artist's role is to help you translate your learning into digestible pieces to share with your community.
  • Artistry includes spoken word artists, zine artists, puppet artists, and print artists. Projects might include zine, coloring book, children’s book, video, teach in etc.
